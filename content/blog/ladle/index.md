## Meaning

A **ladle** is a large, deep spoon with a long handle, used especially for serving liquids such as soup, stew, or sauce.
It allows you to scoop and pour hot liquids easily from a pot or bowl.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun
- **Countable:** Yes (e.g., _a ladle_, _two ladles of soup_)
- Often used with verbs like _use_, _fill_, _serve_, or _pour_.
- Common structure:

  - _ladle of + liquid_ → _a ladle of soup_, _two ladles of stew_
  - _with a ladle_ → _She served the soup with a ladle._

## Common Phrases

- **soup ladle** – a ladle specifically used for soup.
- **a ladle of (something)** – a portion measured by one ladle.
- **to ladle out** – to serve or pour with a ladle.

  - _She ladled out hot chocolate for everyone._

## Collocations

- **use a ladle**
- **dip a ladle into**
- **fill a ladle with**
- **serve with a ladle**
- **a metal/plastic ladle**

## Examples

1. She used a **ladle** to serve the soup into bowls.
2. He poured a **ladle of sauce** over the pasta.
3. The chef dipped the **ladle** into the pot and tasted the broth.
4. Please **ladle out** some stew for the guests.
5. A wooden **ladle** was hanging next to the large cooking pot.
6. He filled a **ladle** with hot curry and poured it over the rice.
7. She gently **ladled** the punch into glasses.
8. The **soup ladle** is kept in the top drawer of the kitchen.
9. He took another **ladle** of the hearty chicken stew.
10. The cook used a **metal ladle** to stir the boiling mixture.
