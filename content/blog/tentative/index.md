## Meaning

_Tentative_ means **not fully decided, fixed, or certain** — something that is **subject to change** or **done as a trial**.
It can also describe actions that are **hesitant** or **uncertain**.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- **Common patterns:**

  - _tentative plan/decision/schedule_ → not final or confirmed
  - _tentative smile/move_ → showing hesitation or lack of confidence

- Often used in business, science, or planning contexts to express **temporary or provisional** ideas.

## Common Phrases

- tentative plan
- tentative schedule
- tentative agreement
- tentative steps
- tentative conclusion

## Collocations

- **make a tentative plan**
- **give a tentative answer**
- **reach a tentative agreement**
- **take a tentative step**
- **tentative date/time**

## Examples

1. We made a **tentative plan** to meet next Friday.
2. The company reached a **tentative agreement** with the labor union.
3. She gave a **tentative smile**, unsure of how to respond.
4. The results are **tentative** and need further testing.
5. Our **tentative schedule** may change depending on the client’s feedback.
6. He took a **tentative step** into the dark room.
7. The scientists formed a **tentative hypothesis** based on limited data.
8. The project’s **tentative deadline** is next month.
9. His voice was **tentative**, as if afraid to speak too loudly.
10. Let’s set a **tentative date** for the meeting and adjust later if needed.

## Synonyms or Related

- definite
- certain
- final
