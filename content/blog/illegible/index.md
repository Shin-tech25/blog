## Meaning

**Illegible** means _not clear enough to be read_.
It is used when handwriting, text, or print is so unclear, messy, or faded that people cannot read it easily.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- **Typical structure:**

  - _illegible handwriting / signature / note_
  - _be illegible to someone_
  - _make something illegible_

**Example pattern:**

- _The ink has faded, and the words are illegible._
- _His handwriting is almost illegible._

## Common Phrases

- **illegible handwriting** – writing that is hard to read
- **illegible signature** – a signature that cannot be deciphered
- **nearly illegible text** – writing that is barely readable

## Collocations

| Noun        | Example                      |
| ----------- | ---------------------------- |
| handwriting | illegible handwriting        |
| signature   | an illegible signature       |
| note        | an illegible note            |
| writing     | completely illegible writing |

## Examples

1. His handwriting was so **illegible** that no one could understand the message.
2. The old document became **illegible** due to water damage.
3. Please rewrite this; your notes are **almost illegible**.
4. The fax copy was **blurry and illegible**.
5. She signed her name with an **illegible scrawl**.
6. The teacher couldn’t read the essay because the text was **illegible**.
7. The doctor’s prescription was **completely illegible** to the pharmacist.
