## Meaning

- **Custody** means the legal right or duty to take care of someone or something.
  It often refers to the **guardianship of a child** or **the state of being detained by the police**.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun (uncountable or countable depending on context)
- **Common structures:**

  - _have custody of [someone]_ – to have legal guardianship.
  - _be in custody_ – to be detained by the police or authorities.
  - _gain/lose custody_ – to win or lose the right to care for a child.

## Common Phrases

- **child custody** – legal responsibility for a child.
- **custody battle** – a legal dispute over who gets custody.
- **joint custody** – shared guardianship between parents.
- **police custody** – detention by law enforcement.
- **protective custody** – when someone is kept in detention for their safety.

## Collocations

- **gain custody**, **lose custody**, **award custody**, **take into custody**, **be held in custody**, **in police custody**, **custody arrangement**.

## Examples

1. The mother was granted **custody** of her two children after the divorce.
2. He spent the night in **police custody** after being arrested.
3. The parents are fighting a long **custody battle** in court.
4. The suspect remains **in custody** while awaiting trial.
5. They agreed on **joint custody** to ensure both parents stay involved.
6. The court **awarded custody** to the father.
7. She was taken into **protective custody** for her own safety.
8. After the verdict, the defendant was **taken into custody** immediately.
9. The lawyer specializes in **custody cases**.
10. The child’s **custody arrangement** was reviewed by the court.

## Synonyms or Related

- care
- detention
- imprisonment
- control

## Antonym

- freedom
- liberty
