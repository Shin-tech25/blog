## Meaning

**Dilute** means **to make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent**.
It can also mean **to weaken the strength, quality, or value of something** (like an argument, belief, or power).

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** verb / adjective
- **Verb forms:** dilute – diluted – diluted – diluting
- **Adjective form:** dilute (e.g., a dilute solution)
- **Transitive verb:** takes a direct object (e.g., _dilute the juice with water_)

### Common structures

- **dilute + something + with + something**
  → to mix and weaken a liquid
  _Example: She diluted the vinegar with water._
- **dilute + something**
  → to weaken or lessen the strength or impact
  _Example: The company diluted its brand image by lowering product quality._

## Common Phrases

- **dilute solution** – a weak mixture
- **dilute acid** – an acid that has been mixed with water
- **dilute concentration** – a low level of a substance in a mixture
- **dilute the effect** – reduce or weaken the effect

## Collocations

- **verb + dilute:** try to dilute, need to dilute, must dilute
- **noun + dilute:** water, acid, juice, paint, medicine
- **adjective + dilute:** slightly dilute, very dilute

## Examples

1. You should **dilute** the juice with water before giving it to children.
2. The teacher **diluted** the complex idea to make it easier to understand.
3. Investors feared that new shares would **dilute** the value of existing ones.
4. The medicine is too strong; please **dilute** it before use.
5. The report **dilutes** the seriousness of the problem.
6. A **dilute** acid is less dangerous but still must be handled carefully.
7. They tried to **dilute** the criticism with humor.
8. Adding ice cubes will **dilute** your drink over time.

## Synonyms or Related
