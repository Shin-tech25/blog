## Meaning

_“Retrieve”_ means **to get something back**, **to recover**, or **to bring something back to its original place or state**. It can refer to both physical objects and abstract things like data or memories.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb
- **Verb forms:** retrieve – retrieved – retrieved – retrieving
- **Transitivity:** Transitive (requires an object)

**Typical patterns:**

- retrieve something
- retrieve something from somewhere
- retrieve data/information/files

## Common Phrases

- retrieve data
- retrieve information
- retrieve a file
- retrieve a lost item
- retrieve a situation (make it better again)

## Collocations

- **verb + retrieve:** try to retrieve, manage to retrieve, help to retrieve
- **noun + retrieve:** data retrieval, information retrieval, memory retrieval

## Examples

1. She managed to **retrieve her wallet** from the lost and found office.
2. The dog was trained to **retrieve the ball** from the water.
3. The technician **retrieved the deleted files** from the backup system.
4. He tried to **retrieve his reputation** after the scandal.
5. The police **retrieved the stolen artwork** from the thieves.
6. I couldn’t **retrieve the email** because the server was down.
7. Memory exercises can help **retrieve old memories** more easily.
8. The company is working to **retrieve customer trust** after the data breach.

## Synonyms or Related

- recover
- regain
- reclaim
- fetch
- get back

## Antonym

- lose
- misplace
- forfeit
