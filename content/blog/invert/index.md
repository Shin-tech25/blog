## Meaning

_To invert_ means **to turn something upside down or to reverse the position, order, or direction of something**.
It can refer to physical objects, processes, or abstract ideas like logic or relationships.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb
- **Verb type:** Transitive (takes an object) / Intransitive (sometimes used without an object)
- **Common structure:**

  - _invert + noun_
  - _invert something to + form_

**Examples of structures:**

- _invert the colors_
- _invert the order of the list_
- _invert the triangle so that the base is on top_

## Common Phrases

- **invert the order** – to reverse the sequence.
- **invert the colors** – to swap light and dark areas (commonly used in digital editing).
- **invert logic** – to reverse reasoning or cause and effect.
- **invert a fraction** – to flip numerator and denominator.

## Collocations

- **invert** + position/order/relationship
- **invert** + structure/logic/signal
- **mathematically invert**
- **color-invert mode**

## Examples

1. The teacher asked the students to **invert the fraction** to find its reciprocal.
2. The artist **inverted the colors** of the photo for a dramatic effect.
3. You need to **invert the cup** before stacking it.
4. The scientist **inverted the test tube** to mix the chemicals.
5. If you **invert the order** of the steps, the process won’t work.
6. The company’s pyramid structure was **inverted**, giving more power to the workers.
7. In logic, to **invert a statement** means to express the opposite condition.
8. The camera app has an **invert colors** option for accessibility.
