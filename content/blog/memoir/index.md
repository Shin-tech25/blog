## Meaning

A **memoir** is a written account of someone's personal experiences or memories, often focusing on particular events, themes, or periods in their life.
It is similar to an autobiography but usually covers specific aspects rather than an entire life story.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun
- **Countable/Uncountable:** Countable (usually singular)
- **Typical structure:**

  - write a memoir
  - publish a memoir about/of something

**Examples:**

- _She wrote a memoir about her years as a war correspondent._
- _His memoirs detail his journey from poverty to success._

## Common Phrases

- **write a memoir** – to compose a written account of one’s life experiences
- **publish a memoir** – to release it publicly or in print
- **memoir of childhood** – focusing on early life memories
- **political memoir** – written by a politician about their career and decisions

## Collocations

| Verb + Memoir                 | Adjective + Memoir                    | Memoir + of                        |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| write, publish, read, release | personal, political, touching, honest | memoir of childhood, memoir of war |

## Examples

1. She’s currently writing a **memoir** about her experiences living abroad.
2. The general’s **memoirs** became a bestseller.
3. His **memoir** reveals details about his relationship with famous artists.
4. Reading her **memoir**, I felt as if I were reliving her journey.
5. The author’s **memoir** of survival inspired millions around the world.
6. He plans to publish his **memoirs** after retirement.
7. The **memoir** gives a deeply personal perspective on historical events.
8. Her **memoir** focuses on the lessons she learned during her illness.
