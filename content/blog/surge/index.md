## Meaning

**Surge** means a sudden and strong movement or increase. It can describe physical motion (like waves or crowds) or metaphorical rises (like emotions or prices).

As a **verb**, it means _to rise or move suddenly and powerfully_.
As a **noun**, it refers to _a sudden strong increase or rush_.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Verb (intransitive)**: _to move quickly and powerfully forward or upward._

  - Structure: _surge + forward/up/through etc._
  - Example: _The crowd surged forward._

- **Noun**: _a sudden strong movement or increase._

  - Structure: _a surge of + noun_ (e.g., _a surge of anger_, _a surge in prices_)

### Common verb patterns

- _surge forward / up / into / through_
- _a surge in demand / prices / energy_
- _a surge of emotion / power / excitement_

## Common Phrases

- **surge in demand** – a sudden increase in how much people want something
- **surge of emotion** – a sudden strong feeling
- **power surge** – a sudden increase in electrical power
- **surge forward** – move suddenly ahead

## Collocations

- **verb + surge**: _prices surge, emotions surge, waves surge_
- **noun + surge**: _price surge, energy surge, crowd surge_
- **adjective + surge**: _sudden surge, massive surge, emotional surge_

## Examples

1. The crowd **surged forward** when the doors opened.
2. There was a **surge in demand** for electric vehicles.
3. A **surge of anger** rushed through him when he heard the news.
4. Electricity bills rose after a **power surge** damaged the system.
5. The river **surged over** its banks after heavy rain.
6. Hope **surged** in her heart as she saw the results.
7. The company’s profits **surged** by 30% last quarter.
8. Panic **surged through** the crowd during the blackout.
9. There was a **surge of excitement** as the concert began.
10. Inflation caused a **surge in prices** across the country.
