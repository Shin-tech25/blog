## Meaning

**Attrition** is a noun that means a gradual reduction in the number, strength, or effectiveness of something, especially through **natural or continuous loss** (such as employees leaving a company, customers declining, or resources wearing away).

In business, “attrition” often refers to **employee turnover** — the rate at which employees leave a company and are not immediately replaced.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun
- **Uncountable noun** (usually used without “a”)
- Common structures:

  - _employee attrition rate_
  - _attrition through natural causes_
  - _reduce attrition by improving job satisfaction_

### Types of Attrition (Business Context)

- **Voluntary attrition**: Employees choose to leave.
- **Involuntary attrition**: Employees are terminated or laid off.
- **Natural attrition**: Positions are left unfilled after departures.

## Examples

1. The company is facing a high **employee attrition rate** due to poor management.
2. HR implemented new policies to **reduce attrition** and improve morale.
3. **Customer attrition** has increased since the competitor launched a similar product.
4. The **attrition of troops** during the long battle weakened the army.
5. Over time, **natural attrition** reduced the workforce by 10%.
6. Colleges often experience some level of **student attrition** each semester.
7. The manager analyzed the **attrition data** to identify retention problems.
8. In marketing, **customer attrition** is also called “churn.”
9. The company plans to cut costs through **attrition rather than layoffs**.
10. The **attrition of materials** in the machine led to frequent repairs.
