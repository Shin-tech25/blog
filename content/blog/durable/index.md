## Meaning

**Durable** means **able to last for a long time without breaking, wearing out, or losing quality**.
It describes something **strong, long-lasting, and resistant to damage**.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- **Comparative:** more durable
- **Superlative:** most durable

**Typical structures:**

- _durable + noun_ → “durable materials”, “durable shoes”
- _be durable enough to + verb_ → “The fabric is durable enough to withstand heavy use.”

## Common Phrases

- **durable goods** – items intended to last for a long time (e.g., cars, furniture)
- **highly durable** – extremely long-lasting
- **durable material** – strong substance that resists wear and tear
- **durable relationship** – long-lasting connection or bond

## Collocations

- **durable** + **material/fabric/product/structure/shoes/relationship**
- **highly/extremely/remarkably** + **durable**

## Examples

1. These boots are made of **durable leather** that can last for years.
2. The company focuses on producing **durable and eco-friendly** products.
3. Our friendship has proven to be **durable** despite many challenges.
4. This table is very **durable**, even after years of use.
5. Scientists developed a **durable** plastic that resists heat and chemicals.
6. A **durable peace** is one that lasts through generations.
7. The phone’s case is **durable enough to survive** several drops.
8. The watch’s **durable design** makes it suitable for outdoor activities.

## Synonyms or Related

- sturdy

- hard-wearing

## Antonym

- fragile
- weak
