## Meaning

**Menace** means something or someone that is dangerous or likely to cause harm. It can also refer to the act of threatening or being a threat.
As a verb, **to menace** means “to threaten” or “to pose a danger to.”

- **Noun:** a person or thing that is likely to cause harm or danger.
- **Verb:** to threaten harm or cause fear.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun / Verb
- **Common noun pattern:** _a menace to someone/something_
  e.g., “Air pollution is a menace to health.”
- **Verb pattern:** _menace someone/something_
  e.g., “The storm menaced the coastline.”
- Can be used both **literally** (actual danger) and **figuratively** (emotional or social threat).

## Common Phrases

- **a menace to society** – someone who causes trouble or danger to others.
- **pose a menace** – to present a threat or danger.
- **under menace of** – being threatened or forced by danger.
- **menace with** – to threaten someone using something (e.g., “He menaced her with a knife.”)

## Collocations

- **serious menace**
- **potential menace**
- **menace to public safety**
- **pose a menace**
- **menacing look / tone / glare**

## Examples

1. Air pollution is a serious **menace** to human health.
2. The barking dog was a real **menace** to the neighborhood.
3. The gang’s activities **menace** the peace of the city.
4. A dark figure **menaced** them from the shadows.
5. Some teenagers see boredom as a **menace** to their happiness.
6. The dictator **menaced** his enemies with imprisonment.
7. Drunk driving is a **menace** on the roads.
8. The menacing clouds signaled that a storm was approaching.
9. The criminal was described as a **menace to society**.
10. His eyes **menaced** her with silent anger.
