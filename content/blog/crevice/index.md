## Meaning

A **crevice** is a **narrow crack or opening**, especially in a rock, wall, or other hard surface. It is smaller than a fissure and often deep.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun
- **Countable:** Yes (plural: _crevices_)
- Used to describe small splits or cracks, particularly those formed naturally or through wear.
- Often used in geology, construction, and everyday speech.

**Structure examples:**

- _a crevice in the wall_
- _deep crevices between the rocks_

## Common Phrases

- **rock crevice** – a narrow opening in rocks.
- **ice crevice** – a crack in a glacier or frozen surface.
- **fill the crevice** – to seal or cover a small opening.
- **hidden in the crevice** – something placed or concealed in a small crack.

## Collocations

- **deep crevice**
- **tiny crevice**
- **narrow crevice**
- **crevice in the ground**
- **dust/dirt in the crevice**

## Examples

1. Water trickled down through the crevices in the rocks.
2. The gecko hid in a small crevice between the stones.
3. Years of weathering created deep crevices on the mountain face.
4. He found a coin lodged in a crevice of the old wall.
5. Ice had formed inside the crevices of the pavement.
6. Plants can sometimes grow in the tiniest crevices of cliffs.
7. The explorer carefully examined each crevice for fossils.
8. Dust often collects in the crevices of wooden furniture.
