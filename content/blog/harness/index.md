## Meaning

**Harness** has two main meanings:

1. **(noun)** Equipment of straps and fittings used to control or attach an animal (especially a horse) to something like a cart or plow.
2. **(verb)** To control and make use of something, especially a natural resource or energy.

## Grammar and Usage

**Part of speech:**

- _Verb_ (transitive) — needs an object.
- _Noun_ — countable.

**Typical verb pattern:**

- harness + energy/power/resource
- harness + creativity/technology/data

**Example patterns:**

- _to harness solar energy_
- _to harness the power of AI_
- _to harness a horse to a cart_

## Common Phrases

- **harness energy** – to collect and use energy efficiently.
- **harness potential** – to make full use of someone’s or something’s abilities.
- **safety harness** – a device worn to prevent falling from heights.
- **harness the wind** – to generate power from wind energy.

## Collocations

- **verb + harness**: use, wear, fasten, attach
- **noun + harness**: power harness, safety harness, leather harness
- **harness + noun**: energy, technology, data, resources, potential

## Examples

1. Engineers are trying to **harness** the power of the ocean waves.
2. The company hopes to **harness** artificial intelligence to improve efficiency.
3. She wore a **safety harness** while climbing the tower.
4. Farmers used to **harness** horses to pull heavy loads.
5. We need to **harness** our creativity to solve this problem.
6. The new system allows users to **harness** real-time data.
7. Solar panels **harness** sunlight to produce electricity.
8. The **harness** around the dog was adjusted for comfort.
