## Meaning

_Radiant_ means **shining or glowing brightly**. It can describe both **literal brightness** (like sunlight) and **figurative brightness** (like happiness or beauty that seems to shine out from someone).

It conveys the sense of **light, warmth, or joy spreading outward**.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- **Comparative:** more radiant
- **Superlative:** most radiant
- **Usage patterns:**

  - “be radiant with + emotion” → shows emotion glowing outward
    e.g., _She was radiant with happiness._
  - “a radiant + noun” → describes something bright or shining
    e.g., _a radiant smile_, _a radiant morning_, _a radiant face_

## Common Phrases

- **radiant smile** – a bright, happy smile
- **radiant beauty** – glowing, stunning beauty
- **radiant energy** – energy that travels as waves (scientific use)
- **radiant sunlight** – shining sunlight
- **radiant with joy** – visibly full of joy

## Examples

1. The bride looked **radiant** in her white gown.
2. Her face was **radiant with joy** after hearing the good news.
3. The **radiant sunlight** filled the room with warmth.
4. He gave me a **radiant smile** that brightened my day.
5. The garden was **radiant with color** in spring.
6. She seemed **radiant** despite the long, tiring day.
7. The scientist studied the **radiant energy** emitted by the sun.
8. His **radiant confidence** inspired everyone around him.
