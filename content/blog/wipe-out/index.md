## Meaning

**Wipe out** means to **completely destroy**, **eliminate**, or **remove something or someone**. It can also mean **to fall down hard** (especially in surfing or sports).

## Grammar and Usage

- **Verb type:** Phrasal verb (transitive or intransitive)
- **Structure:** _wipe out + noun_ / _be wiped out_
- **Common meanings:**

  1. To destroy or eliminate completely
     → “The disease wiped out half the population.”
  2. To exhaust or tire someone completely
     → “The long hike really wiped me out.”
  3. To fall or crash badly (informal, especially in sports)
     → “He wiped out while surfing a huge wave.”

## Common Phrases

- **wipe out a debt** — to pay off or eliminate debt
- **wipe out a species** — to make an animal species extinct
- **completely wiped out** — totally exhausted or destroyed
- **wipe out the competition** — to defeat opponents completely

## Collocations

- **wipe out** + _disease / population / debt / species / enemy_
- _completely / almost / totally_ **wiped out**
- **be wiped out by** _something_

## Examples

1. The tsunami wiped out several coastal villages.
2. A new vaccine could wipe out the virus entirely.
3. I’m completely wiped out after working 12 hours straight.
4. The army was nearly wiped out in the battle.
5. He wiped out on his skateboard and broke his arm.
6. The company’s debts were wiped out after the merger.
7. Deforestation is wiping out natural habitats.
8. The marathon runners looked totally wiped out at the finish line.

## Synonyms or Related
