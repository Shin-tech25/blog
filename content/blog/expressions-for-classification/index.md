## Meaning

This article explains expressions used to show **classification or grouping**, such as **be categorized into**, **be classified as**, and **fall into**.
They all describe the act of organizing things or people into specific **groups, types, or categories** based on shared characteristics.

## Grammar and Usage

These expressions usually appear in the **passive voice**, because the subject (thing/person) is being classified by someone or something else.

- **be categorized into + plural noun** → grouped into categories
- **be classified as + noun** → assigned to a specific type or label
- **fall into + category/type** → naturally belong to a certain group

Examples:

- Books in the library are **categorized into** sections.
- The whale **is classified as** a mammal.
- These issues **fall into** three main types.

## Comparison and Mapping

| Expression              | Core Meaning                                 | Formality  | Typical Use                         | Example                                            |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ---------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **be categorized into** | divided into several distinct categories     | medium     | general use, academic writing       | The products are **categorized into** four groups. |
| **be classified as**    | officially identified as a type              | high       | science, law, formal classification | This species **is classified as** endangered.      |
| **fall into**           | naturally belong to a type or group          | medium     | general and natural grouping        | These problems **fall into** two main categories.  |
| **be grouped into**     | gathered and divided based on features       | medium     | general, organizational             | Students are **grouped into** three teams.         |
| **be divided into**     | physically or logically separated into parts | medium     | general, structural                 | The report **is divided into** five sections.      |
| **be sorted into**      | arranged by certain criteria                 | medium–low | data, lists, practical contexts     | Files are **sorted into** folders by date.         |
| **be assigned to**      | officially placed in a category or group     | high       | roles, systems, tasks               | Each user **is assigned to** a permission group.   |
