## Meaning

_“Spectacular”_ means **very impressive, exciting, or eye-catching**.
It describes something that attracts attention because it is **beautiful, dramatic, or remarkable**.

## Grammar and Usage

- Part of speech: **Adjective**
- Meaning: impressive to look at or remarkable in performance or quality
- Common structure:

  - **a spectacular view/event/performance/success**
  - **something looks spectacular**

- It can also be used as a **noun** meaning “a grand show or performance.”

> Example: _The fireworks were a spectacular._ (noun)

## Common Phrases

- spectacular view
- spectacular success
- spectacular failure
- spectacular achievement
- spectacular scenery
- spectacular fireworks

## Collocations

- **make a spectacular comeback**
- **produce a spectacular result**
- **achieve a spectacular victory**
- **look spectacular in** (dress, outfit, etc.)
- **spectacular rise/fall** (in prices, reputation, etc.)

## Examples

1. The mountain offers a **spectacular view** of the valley below.
2. The concert ended with a **spectacular display** of fireworks.
3. Her performance was **spectacular**, earning her a standing ovation.
4. The company made a **spectacular recovery** after last year’s losses.
5. We witnessed a **spectacular sunset** over the ocean.
6. His plan failed in a **spectacular way**, costing millions.
7. The movie’s **spectacular effects** amazed the audience.
8. The athlete made a **spectacular comeback** after an injury.
9. They achieved **spectacular results** in a very short time.
10. The city is famous for its **spectacular festivals** and parades.

## Synonyms or Related

- stunning

- dazzling

## Antonym

- ordinary
- dull
- unimpressive
