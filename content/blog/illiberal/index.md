## Meaning

**Illiberal** means **not generous, narrow-minded, or opposed to freedom**. It often describes a person, idea, or system that restricts freedom of thought or behavior and lacks tolerance toward others’ views.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- Used to describe people, actions, or policies.
- Common structures:

  - _illiberal attitude / view / policy / society_
  - _be illiberal toward someone / something_

It can refer to:

1. **Lack of freedom** (e.g., authoritarian systems).
2. **Lack of generosity or tolerance** (e.g., narrow-minded individuals).
3. **Lack of broad education or taste** (archaic usage).

## Common Phrases

- **illiberal democracy** – a government that holds elections but restricts civil liberties.
- **illiberal attitude** – an intolerant or closed-minded way of thinking.
- **illiberal policy** – a policy limiting freedom or equality.

## Collocations

- **illiberal regime / government**
- **illiberal thinking / mentality**
- **illiberal society / system**
- **illiberal behavior / laws**
- **illiberal toward critics / minorities**

## Examples

1. The country’s **illiberal government** suppresses free speech and the press.
2. His **illiberal attitude** toward other religions caused many disagreements.
3. Critics warned that the new law represents an **illiberal shift** in politics.
4. She was surprisingly **illiberal** for someone so well-educated.
5. The organization condemned the **illiberal measures** imposed by the authorities.
6. An **illiberal democracy** often maintains elections but undermines human rights.
7. Their **illiberal policies** discourage creativity and innovation.
8. The leader’s **illiberal stance** drew international criticism.
9. Education should combat **illiberal thinking**.
10. The novel portrays an **illiberal society** that fears change.
