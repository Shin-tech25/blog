## Meaning

**Fracture** means a **break or crack** in a hard material, especially in a **bone** or **rock**.
As a verb, it means **to break** or **cause something to break**.

- **Noun:** a break or crack (e.g., a bone fracture)
- **Verb:** to break (something) or cause a break (e.g., to fracture your arm)

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:**

  - _Noun:_ countable/uncountable (e.g., _a bone fracture_, _multiple fractures_)
  - _Verb:_ transitive/intransitive (e.g., _She fractured her leg._ / _The rock fractured easily._)

- **Typical patterns:**

  - _fracture + bone/structure/material_
  - _be/get fractured_ (passive form is common)

## Common Phrases

- **bone fracture** – a break in a bone
- **stress fracture** – a small crack in a bone caused by repeated stress
- **compound fracture** – a severe break where the bone pierces the skin
- **fracture of the spine/arm/skull**
- **hairline fracture** – a very thin crack in a bone or material

## Collocations

- **verb + fracture:** suffer, cause, detect, treat, heal, repair
- **adjective + fracture:** minor, severe, compound, clean, multiple
- **noun + fracture:** bone, skull, wrist, ankle, rock

## Examples

1. The X-ray showed a small **fracture** in her wrist.
2. He **fractured** his ankle while playing basketball.
3. The company’s unity began to **fracture** after the scandal.
4. Repeated pressure can cause a **stress fracture** in the foot.
5. The bone will take six weeks to **heal** after the **fracture**.
6. The earthquake **fractured** several old buildings.
7. Doctors used metal plates to **stabilize the fractured bone**.
8. Even a **hairline fracture** can be painful and require rest.

## Synonyms or Related

- **break**
- **crack**
- **split**
- **shatter**
- **rupture**

## Antonym

- **heal**
- **mend**
- **repair**
