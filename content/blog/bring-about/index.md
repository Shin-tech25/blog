## Meaning

**Bring about** means _to cause something to happen_ or _to make something come into existence_.
It is often used when referring to changes, results, or outcomes brought by someoneâ€™s actions or certain events.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Phrasal verb
- **Transitivity:** Transitive (requires an object)
- **Structure:** `bring about + noun`

**Typical pattern:**

- bring about change/improvement/results/reform

It is used in both formal and informal contexts.

## Common Phrases

- bring about change
- bring about peace
- bring about a revolution
- bring about reform
- bring about improvements

## Collocations

- **noun + bring about**: policy that brings about
- **verb + bring about**: help/attempt/try to bring about
- **adjective + noun**: significant change brought about

## Examples

1. The new policy **brought about** major improvements in the education system.
2. The internet **brought about** a revolution in communication.
3. His speech **brought about** a change in public opinion.
4. The storm **brought about** severe damage to the coastal areas.
5. Technological innovation has **brought about** economic growth.
6. The peace talks **brought about** the end of the conflict.
7. Environmental awareness campaigns can **bring about** positive change.
8. A small mistake **brought about** the failure of the entire plan.
