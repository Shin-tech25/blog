## Meaning

**Siege** means a **military operation** in which armed forces surround a place, cutting off essential supplies, to force those inside to surrender.
It can also mean a **persistent or prolonged effort or pressure** in non-military contexts.

_(Part of speech: noun and verb)_

## Grammar and Usage

- **As a noun:**
  “a siege of a city,” “under siege,” “lay siege to something.”
  → Refers to the act of surrounding and attacking a fortified place.
- **As a verb (rare in modern use):**
  “to siege a castle” — to surround and attack it.

### Common structures

- **lay siege to + place**
- **be under siege**
- **a siege of + (emotion/effort)** (figurative use)

## Common Phrases

- **lay siege to a city** — to surround a city to capture it
- **be under siege** — to be surrounded or attacked continuously
- **a siege mentality** — a defensive attitude caused by feeling constantly attacked or criticized

## Collocations

- **verb + siege:** lay, endure, lift, break
- **noun + siege:** city, fortress, castle, army, resistance
- **adjective + siege:** long, brutal, military, psychological

## Examples

1. The army laid **siege** to the fortress for several months.
2. The castle was **under siege** until the defenders surrendered.
3. The rebels **lifted the siege** after a peace agreement.
4. The company has developed a **siege mentality** after years of criticism.
5. The medieval **siege** lasted through the entire winter.
6. The city was **besieged** by enemy forces on all sides.
7. The police laid **siege** to the building where the suspect was hiding.
8. The small garrison **held out** during the long **siege**.
9. The town’s residents suffered hunger during the **siege**.
10. After months of **siege**, the gates finally fell.
