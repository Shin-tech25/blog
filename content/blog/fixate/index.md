## Meaning

**Fixate** means to become **focused or obsessed** with something or someone, often in an **excessive or unhealthy way**.
It can also mean to **focus one’s eyes or attention** on something firmly.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb
- **Forms:** fixate, fixates, fixated, fixating
- **Common structures:**

  - **fixate on/upon something** — to obsessively focus attention on something
  - **be fixated on/upon something** — to describe a state of obsession

**Examples of patterns:**

- _He fixated on the smallest details._
- _She is fixated on her appearance._

## Common Phrases

- **fixate on the past** – to be unable to move on from previous events
- **fixate on success** – to be overly focused on achieving success
- **fixate on someone** – to become emotionally or mentally obsessed with a person

## Collocations

- **fixate on details**
- **fixate on mistakes**
- **fixate on perfection**
- **be fixated with technology**
- **be fixated by fame**

## Examples

1. He tends to **fixate on** minor problems instead of seeing the bigger picture.
2. The media **fixated on** her relationship rather than her achievements.
3. She’s completely **fixated on** staying young.
4. Don’t **fixate on** what went wrong; think about how to improve.
5. As a child, he **fixated on** dinosaurs and learned every fact about them.
6. The fans were **fixated by** the celebrity’s private life.
7. Some people **fixate on** numbers instead of human outcomes.
8. He became **fixated with** the idea of becoming rich overnight.
9. She couldn’t help but **fixate on** the clock, waiting for the meeting to end.
10. The artist **fixated on** capturing light and shadow in his paintings.

## Synonyms or Related

- preoccupy

## Antonym

- overlook
- ignore
- disregard
