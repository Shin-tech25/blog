## Meaning

**Imprisonment** means the state of being put in prison as a punishment or by legal authority. It refers to confinement or restriction of freedom, typically after being convicted of a crime.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun (uncountable)
- **Related verb:** _imprison_ (to put someone in prison)
- **Typical structure:**

  - _be sentenced to imprisonment_
  - _life imprisonment_, _imprisonment for X years_

## Common Phrases

- **life imprisonment** – a punishment of spending the rest of one’s life in prison
- **term of imprisonment** – the length of time someone must stay in prison
- **false imprisonment** – the illegal confinement of a person without lawful reason

## Collocations

- **serve imprisonment**
- **sentence to imprisonment**
- **release from imprisonment**
- **face imprisonment**
- **imprisonment term / duration**

## Examples

1. He was sentenced to **five years’ imprisonment** for robbery.
2. The new law reduced the maximum **term of imprisonment** for minor offenses.
3. She faced **life imprisonment** for her involvement in the crime.
4. The activist was released after two years of **wrongful imprisonment**.
5. **Imprisonment** is often seen as a last resort for non-violent crimes.
6. He avoided **imprisonment** by agreeing to community service.
7. The court ruled it was a case of **false imprisonment**.
8. They campaign against unfair **imprisonment** of political prisoners.
9. His long **imprisonment** changed his perspective on life.
10. The judge warned that repeat offenses could lead to **imprisonment**.

- incarceration

## Antonym
