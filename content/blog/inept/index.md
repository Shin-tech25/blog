## Meaning

_Inept_ means **having or showing no skill; clumsy or incompetent**.
It describes a person who is **bad at doing something** or **lacks ability or judgment** in a specific situation.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- **Typical patterns:**

  - _inept at (doing) something_ → "He is inept at cooking."
  - _inept in (a field or skill)_ → "She felt inept in social situations."
  - _an inept remark/decision/action_ → modifying a noun.

**Common sentence structures:**

- _Someone is inept at doing something._
- _It was an inept attempt/decision/remark._

## Common Phrases

- **inept at handling situations**
- **an inept performance**
- **an inept leader**
- **socially inept**
- **inept decision-making**

## Collocations

- **Verb + inept:** seem, appear, look
- **Adverb + inept:** completely, totally, utterly, hopelessly
- **Noun + inept:** inept handling, inept performance, inept remark

## Examples

1. He was completely **inept at** managing people.
2. The company suffered from **inept leadership**.
3. Her **inept handling** of the situation only made things worse.
4. He made an **inept remark** that offended everyone at the table.
5. Despite his enthusiasm, he was **socially inept** and shy.
6. The team's **inept defense** led to their defeat.
7. That was an **inept attempt** to fix the computer.
8. She felt **inept in** front of experienced professionals.
