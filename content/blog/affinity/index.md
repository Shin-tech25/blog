## Meaning

_Affinity_ means a natural liking, attraction, or connection between people, things, or ideas. It can describe emotional closeness, similarity, or compatibility.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun
- **Countable/Uncountable:** Usually uncountable when referring to a general feeling; countable when referring to a specific connection.
- **Common structure:**

  - _affinity for/to something_ → liking or attraction
  - _affinity with/between A and B_ → similarity or connection

**Examples of patterns:**

- “have an affinity for music”
- “share an affinity with animals”
- “an affinity between two cultures”

## Common Phrases

- **natural affinity** – a strong and natural liking or attraction
- **affinity for art/science** – a liking or talent for a subject
- **cultural affinity** – a sense of closeness between cultures
- **spiritual affinity** – emotional or moral connection

## Collocations

- **strong affinity**
- **deep affinity**
- **close affinity**
- **affinity between**
- **affinity with**
- **affinity for**

## Examples

1. She has a natural **affinity for** languages.
2. There is a strong **affinity between** humans and dogs.
3. I felt an instant **affinity with** her personality.
4. His **affinity for** classical music began in childhood.
5. Scientists found an **affinity between** the two species.
6. Many Japanese people feel a **cultural affinity with** Hawaii.
7. The company values employees with an **affinity for** teamwork.
8. Their shared **affinity for** adventure made them close friends.

## Synonyms or Related

- connection
- sympathy

- compatibility

## Antonym

- aversion
- disconnection
- incompatibility
