## Meaning

**Mischief** refers to behavior or actions that cause minor trouble, annoyance, or harm — often playful rather than seriously harmful. It can describe the fun kind of naughtiness or deliberate troublemaking.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Noun
- **Uncountable noun** in most cases.
- Common structures:

  - _get into mischief_ (do something naughty)
  - _cause mischief_ (create trouble)
  - _full of mischief_ (very playful or naughty)

**Examples of grammatical use:**

- _Children often get into mischief when left alone._
- _Her smile was full of mischief._

## Common Phrases

- **Make mischief** — to cause trouble or discord.
- **Get into mischief** — to behave badly or play tricks.
- **Full of mischief** — having a playful or naughty character.
- **Cause mischief** — to create trouble or problems for others.

## Collocations

- _childish mischief_
- _innocent mischief_
- _playful mischief_
- _act of mischief_
- _spirit of mischief_

## Examples

1. The boy’s eyes sparkled with **mischief** as he hid his friend’s shoes.
2. Don’t get into **mischief** while I’m gone!
3. Her **mischief** often brought laughter to the classroom.
4. The cat was always up to some **mischief** when no one was watching.
5. A little **mischief** can make life more fun.
6. He spread **mischief** by telling lies between friends.
7. Their laughter was a sign of harmless **mischief**.
8. The teacher caught them in the middle of their **mischief**.

## Synonyms or Related

- Playfulness
- Mischievousness

## Antonym

- Discipline
