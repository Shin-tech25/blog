## Meaning

**Proclaim** means to announce something officially, publicly, or in a confident way. It often implies declaring something important or making something known openly.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb
- **Transitivity:** Transitive (takes a direct object)
- **Typical patterns:**

  - **proclaim something** – to announce or declare something.
  - **proclaim someone/something as something** – to officially state someone’s status or role.
  - **proclaim that...** – to formally state or declare a fact or opinion.

**Examples:**

- _The government proclaimed a new policy._
- _They proclaimed her as the winner._
- _He proclaimed that the war was over._

## Common Phrases

- **proclaim independence**
- **proclaim victory**
- **proclaim peace**
- **proclaim someone king/queen**
- **proclaim the gospel**

## Collocations

- **adverb + proclaim:** publicly proclaim, boldly proclaim, officially proclaim
- **verb + noun:** proclaim freedom, proclaim law, proclaim victory, proclaim loyalty

## Examples

1. The president **proclaimed** a state of emergency.
2. The nation **proclaimed** its independence in 1945.
3. The scientist **proclaimed** his discovery to the world.
4. She **proclaimed** her love for him without hesitation.
5. The crowd **proclaimed** the hero’s return.
6. The priest **proclaimed** the message of hope.
7. The company **proclaimed** that it would go carbon-neutral.
8. The city **proclaimed** March 15 as “Clean Earth Day.”
