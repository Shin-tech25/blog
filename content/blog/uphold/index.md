## Meaning

**Uphold** means to **support**, **maintain**, or **defend** something, especially a principle, law, decision, or standard.
It suggests keeping something valid, respected, or in force.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb (transitive)
- **Forms:** uphold – upheld – upheld
- **Typical structures:**

  - _uphold + law / principle / decision / right_
  - _uphold the tradition / constitution / values_

**Examples of sentence structures:**

- The court **upheld the decision** of the lower court.
- We must **uphold the rights** of every citizen.

## Common Phrases

- **uphold justice** – to maintain fairness and legality
- **uphold the law** – to ensure laws are respected and enforced
- **uphold human rights** – to protect fundamental freedoms
- **uphold a tradition** – to continue or preserve customs

## Collocations

| Verb + Noun        | Meaning                          |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| uphold justice     | defend fairness                  |
| uphold a decision  | confirm or support a ruling      |
| uphold values      | maintain beliefs or standards    |
| uphold a complaint | accept that a complaint is valid |

## Examples

1. The Supreme Court **upheld** the ruling made by the district court.
2. She has always **upheld** the principles of honesty and fairness.
3. The organization works to **uphold** human rights worldwide.
4. We must **uphold** our commitment to equality.
5. The judge **upheld** the company’s appeal.
6. They strive to **uphold** traditional values in their community.
7. The school aims to **uphold** a high standard of education.
8. The board **upheld** the disciplinary action after reviewing the case.
