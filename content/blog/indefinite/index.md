## Meaning

**Indefinite** means **not clear, fixed, or limited in meaning, time, or amount**.
It describes something that is **uncertain**, **not specific**, or **without a clear end**.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- Used to describe nouns that are vague or not clearly defined.
- Common structures:

  - **an indefinite period/time** (not clearly limited)
  - **an indefinite answer** (not clearly defined or certain)
  - **an indefinite number of people** (not specific)

**Derived forms:**

- _indefinitely_ (adverb): without a fixed limit of time or extent.

## Common Phrases

- **for an indefinite period** — for an unknown or unlimited time.
- **remain indefinite** — to stay uncertain or unclear.
- **an indefinite plan** — a plan that is not clearly decided or fixed.

## Collocations

- indefinite period
- indefinite leave
- indefinite suspension
- indefinite number
- indefinite article (in grammar: _a_, _an_)

## Examples

1. The project has been postponed **for an indefinite period**.
2. She gave an **indefinite answer** when asked about her future plans.
3. The meeting was **indefinitely delayed** due to technical issues.
4. He has taken **indefinite leave** from work for personal reasons.
5. The term “some” refers to an **indefinite quantity**.
6. Our travel date is still **indefinite** because we’re waiting for visa approval.
7. The government announced an **indefinite suspension** of the program.
8. They decided to live abroad **indefinitely**.
