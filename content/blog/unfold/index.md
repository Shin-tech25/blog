## Meaning

**Unfold** means to **open up something that was folded**, or **to gradually reveal or become clear**.
It can describe both a **physical action** (like opening paper or fabric) and a **figurative process** (like events or stories developing over time).

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb
- **Forms:** unfold – unfolded – unfolded
- **Transitive:** when it takes an object (e.g., _She unfolded the letter._)
- **Intransitive:** when it doesn’t take an object (e.g., _The story unfolded slowly._)

**Patterns:**

- unfold + object → _unfold a map, unfold wings, unfold a secret_
- subject + unfold → _Events unfolded, The truth unfolded_

## Common Phrases

- **Unfold a plan** – reveal or explain a plan in detail
- **As events unfold** – as things happen or develop
- **Unfold one’s arms** – move arms from a crossed position
- **Unfold the truth** – gradually reveal the facts

## Collocations

- **gently unfold**
- **slowly unfold**
- **begin to unfold**
- **unfold like a story**
- **unfold before one’s eyes**

## Examples

1. She **unfolded** the map on the table to show the route.
2. The mystery **unfolded** as the detective revealed each clue.
3. The petals of the flower **unfolded** in the morning sunlight.
4. His plan **unfolded** exactly as he had predicted.
5. As the night **unfolded**, the tension in the room grew.
6. He **unfolded** his arms and sighed deeply.
7. The drama **unfolded** before the audience’s eyes.
8. The teacher **unfolded** the paper crane carefully to show how it was made.
