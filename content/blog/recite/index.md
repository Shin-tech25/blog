## Meaning

_To recite_ means **to say aloud something that has been learned or memorized**, such as a poem, list, or speech.
It can also mean **to list or mention things one by one**.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Verb (transitive or intransitive)
- **Transitive:** “recite something” (to say something from memory)
- **Intransitive:** “recite” (to perform or repeat words aloud)

**Common patterns:**

- recite + poem / speech / prayer / story
- recite something from memory
- recite something to someone

**Example structure:**

> [Subject] + recite + [object] + (from memory / to someone)

## Common Phrases

- **recite a poem** – to say a poem aloud from memory
- **recite from memory** – to recall and speak something without reading
- **recite the alphabet** – to say each letter in order
- **recite the Quran / Bible verses** – to speak religious texts aloud
- **recite a list of names** – to mention names one after another

## Collocations

- **recite fluently / beautifully / perfectly**
- **recite lines / verses / prayers / formulas / facts**
- **recite to the class / audience / teacher**

## Examples

1. The child recited a poem at the school festival.
2. She can recite all the U.S. presidents in order.
3. He recited the prayer softly before going to bed.
4. The student nervously recited her lines on stage.
5. The witness recited the events of that night in detail.
6. We were asked to recite a short passage from memory.
7. The monk recited a verse from the scriptures.
8. He recited the alphabet backward just for fun.

## Synonyms or Related

- **repeat**
- **deliver**
- **rehearse**
- **enumerate**
- **chant**

## Antonym

- **forget**
- **ignore**
- **silence**
