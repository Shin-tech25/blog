## Meaning

**Lucrative** means producing a large amount of money or profit; financially rewarding or profitable.

_Example meaning:_
A _lucrative job_ or _lucrative business_ is one that makes a lot of money.

## Grammar and Usage

- **Part of speech:** Adjective
- **Typical structure:**

  - lucrative + noun
    (e.g., lucrative business, lucrative deal, lucrative opportunity)

- **Used for:** Jobs, markets, businesses, deals, contracts, industries, or investments that bring in significant financial gain.

## Common Phrases

- lucrative business
- lucrative market
- lucrative contract
- lucrative opportunity
- lucrative deal
- lucrative job

## Collocations

| Type                   | Examples                                              |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Noun + lucrative**   | job, career, market, investment, contract             |
| **Verb + lucrative**   | become, prove, remain                                 |
| **Adverb + lucrative** | highly lucrative, extremely lucrative, very lucrative |

## Examples

1. She left her teaching job for a **lucrative position** in finance.
2. The company secured a **lucrative contract** with the government.
3. Real estate can be a **highly lucrative investment** if managed wisely.
4. Many athletes earn **lucrative endorsement deals**.
5. He started a **lucrative online business** during the pandemic.
6. The movie turned out to be a **lucrative franchise** for the studio.
7. They discovered a **lucrative niche** in the tech market.
8. Consulting can be **financially lucrative**, but it often requires long hours.
